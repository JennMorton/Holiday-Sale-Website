Mary Schiller Myers School of Art provides a whole building dedicated to ceramics with five specific rooms. This studio includes an entry level space, an introduction to throwing room, a glaze room, a raw materials room with two clay mixers, a kiln room, and most importantly a wide space for advanced students in which each student has his or her own space.

Most students make their own clay with the options of earthenware, porcelain, or stoneware. An abundant amount of materials are available for using original glazes, stains, or oxides. Students are offered gas, electric, raku, salt, and wood firings, thus giving students real world experience in each process. Students are given a vast variety of materials and processes in which to master.

Overall the Ceramic program lets students independently explore his or her own talent while receiving the guidance of attentive professors. The full list of classes and opportunities are located on the Myers School of Art page.
